About

A project built by people who believe things can be better

We're building a reform movement — not a think tank, not a partisan brand, not a policy library. A movement, built in the open, for everyone.

What We're Building

The Freedom & Dignity Project is a structural reform movement. We're not here to score political points, confirm what you already believe, or produce white papers nobody reads. We're here to build something that lasts: a policy platform grounded in the premise that concentrated power — whether in government, corporations, or a permanent political class — is the root cause of most of what is broken, and that fixing it is the legitimate work of all of us.

We stand in a tradition with deep American roots: the belief that ordinary people deserve structural protection from extraordinary power, and that the work of building a fair society belongs to everyone willing to show up. We're not nostalgic about it. We're not trying to restore any particular era. We're building something new, in our own lane.

What makes this different: we hold serious, substantive positions — on healthcare, labor, housing, the environment, governance — and we refuse to claim those positions as the property of any political identity. We have no ideological purity test. Someone can disagree with one of our pillars and still belong here. What matters is the shared commitment to a better world for everyone, not the credential of already being there.

How to Contribute

There's no one right way to be part of this. Some people organize. Some run for office. Some write policy. Some review proposals. Some shift the culture one conversation at a time. Some donate an hour a week to fact-checking a citation. All of it counts. The door is open — come in at whatever level you can.

Who's Behind This

This project was started by Alice Thomas — a software engineer from Dalton, Georgia, now based in Atlanta. She built it because she watched the country she was taught to believe in fail the test it set for itself. Not dramatically. Slowly, piece by piece, institution by institution. She got fed up, decided to do something about it, and built this. You can too.

Alice is the sole human contributor so far. The research, writing, framing, and policy decisions are hers. That will change as the project grows, and it will be acknowledged here and in the commit history when it does.

A Note on Funding

This project has no outside funding. No donors, no PACs, no grants, no sponsors, no financial backers of any kind. Alice receives no compensation for this work. Every hour is volunteer time. This is, deliberately, a project with no financial obligations to anyone other than herself.

AI tools are used in parts of this research and drafting process. See On the Use of AI for full transparency on how AI is used and what standards govern it.

What We're Not

  • A club that people have to pass a test to join.
  • A lecture. We say what we mean; we don't moralize.
  • A fear machine. We know the problems are serious. We believe they can be fixed.
  • A partisan scoreboard. These values aren't owned by any party.
  • Naive about how hard this is. Or silent about what's wrong.
