This movement grows one person at a time. The Freedom and Dignity Project is building something durable: a constitutional and governance framework that protects rights, limits concentrated power, and guarantees the material conditions for every person to live with dignity. It is not a campaign. It is not a party. It is a framework — built in the open, maintained with evidence, available to anyone.

We are in the early work. That is not a caveat — it is an invitation. Early means the important decisions are still being made, the policy catalog is still being built, and the research is still being gathered. Early means your contribution does not disappear into a bureaucracy. It shapes the thing itself.

There is work here for researchers, writers, policy experts, lawyers, designers, engineers, community organizers, and people who have simply read the platform and want to make it better. There is also work for people who have never opened GitHub in their lives. Whatever your background, there is a place for you here.

There is no single right way to be part of this movement. All of it matters.

The platform holds itself to a high standard of evidence: every factual claim must be sourced, counterevidence is acknowledged, and positions are revised when evidence demands it. That requires human reviewers who know the domain, who can catch errors, and who can push back on weak arguments. That is not something that can be automated.

Come in at whatever level you can. The door is open.

For Technical Contributors

The movement needs engineers — not to maintain a blog, but to build the infrastructure for a serious, long-lived policy platform. Here is what needs to be built:

DB Migration Pipeline
The policy catalog (SQLite) needs a reproducible build pipeline. Source: canonical markdown policy files and structured CSV imports. Output: a validated, version-controlled database with referential integrity.
Static Site Generator
Policy pillar pages are currently hand-authored HTML. They should be generated from the database — a build script that renders templated HTML from structured data, preserving all existing CSS and JS behavior.
Contributor Import Tooling
A workflow for non-technical contributors to submit policy proposals via structured GitHub issues, validated against the catalog schema, and imported through a review queue.
Frontend & Accessibility
WCAG 2.1 AA compliance audit, keyboard navigation testing, screen-reader testing across the full site. Plain-language accessibility for policy content written to ~8th-grade reading level.
