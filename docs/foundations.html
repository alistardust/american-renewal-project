Five Pillars of a Free Society

The Foundations

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

In 1944 Roosevelt named eight rights that Congress never passed. This project finishes that work — and extends it into the century he never lived to see. Five foundations. The architecture of a society that is actually free.

You can't be free if you're sick, homeless, or in debt.
American Renewal Project — in the tradition of Roosevelt, 1944
Foundation I

Accountable Power

The Value: No one is above the law. Power without accountability is not leadership — it is dominance. Every institution in a free society must be answerable to someone other than itself.

Why It Matters: When power concentrates without constraint, everything downstream fails. Corrupt executives escape prosecution. Presidents abuse emergency powers. Judges appointed for life serve partisan agendas. The promise of equal justice becomes fiction. Accountability is not a procedural nicety — it is the load-bearing wall of democratic governance.

What It Demands

  • Enforceable limits on executive power, with independent oversight
  • Immunity for public officials ended — no one is above prosecution
  • Transparent lobbying, procurement, and regulatory processes
  • Judicial accountability: ethics rules, recusal standards, term limits for federal judges
  • Checks and balances restored and made structurally resistant to capture

What It Rejects

  • Presidential immunity doctrines that put the executive above the law
  • Signing statements that effectively nullify legislation
  • Agency heads who serve at the pleasure of the president using that power to bury enforcement
  • Judicial lifetime appointments without accountability mechanisms
  • Corporate officers who externalize harm and walk away unpunished
Executive Power Checks & Balances Term Limits & Fitness Courts & Judicial System Administrative State
Foundation II

Clean Democracy

The Value: Government must answer to people — not to money. The vote must mean something. The institutions that run elections must be insulated from the concentrated private interests they are meant to regulate.

Why It Matters: Money in politics is not free speech — it is structural capture. When billionaires can spend without limit to elect the officials who set the rules of the economy, the democratic feedback loop breaks. Policy drifts toward donor interest and away from public interest. The only antidote is structural: publicly funded elections, enforceable anti-corruption rules, and genuine antitrust enforcement that prevents any single entity from owning too much of the information environment.

What It Demands

  • Public financing of elections — small-dollar matching, public vouchers
  • Dark money ended through disclosure requirements and structural limits
  • Independent redistricting commissions — gerrymandering ended
  • Antitrust enforcement capable of breaking up Big Tech, Big Agriculture, Big Pharma, Big Media
  • Revolving door banned: mandatory cooling-off periods, enforceable and long

What It Rejects

  • Citizens United — corporations are not people and money is not speech
  • Dark money as a legitimate vehicle for political influence
  • Revolving door as accepted practice
  • Gerrymandered maps drawn to produce predetermined outcomes
  • Market concentration that gives a handful of companies control over public discourse
Elections & Representation Anti-Corruption Antitrust & Corporate Power Information & Media
Foundation III

Equal Justice

The Value: The law applies to everyone or it applies to no one. A justice system that produces systematically different outcomes for different people is not a justice system — it is an enforcement system for existing hierarchies.

Why It Matters: Mass incarceration, policing that operates differently by race and ZIP code, an immigration system that creates a permanent underclass of people without rights, and civil courts that grind slowly enough to exhaust anyone without money — these are not bugs in the system. They are features of a system that was never fully reformed. Real freedom requires that the law's promise of equal treatment be made actual, not theoretical.

What It Demands

  • End qualified immunity — officers are accountable for civil rights violations
  • Independent oversight of law enforcement at every level
  • Criminal justice reform: end cash bail, mandatory minimums that produce racial disparity
  • Humane immigration: a functional path to status, family unity, and due process
  • Civil rights enforcement with resources sufficient to make it real

What It Rejects

  • Qualified immunity as a structural shield for officer misconduct
  • Mass detention of immigration cases without due process
  • A sentencing system that produces radically different outcomes by race
  • Prosecutorial discretion used to protect the powerful and punish the weak
  • Underfunded public defense that makes justice contingent on wealth
Equal Justice & Policing Immigration Rights & Civil Liberties
Foundation IV

Real Freedom

The Value: Rights must be explicit, enforceable, and protected in practice. Freedom is not a word in a document — it is a condition of life. Privacy, bodily autonomy, and protection from violence are not negotiable add-ons; they are the minimum conditions for a life with dignity.

Why It Matters: The digital surveillance apparatus built in the last twenty-five years represents the most comprehensive intrusion into private life in human history — and it was built almost entirely by private entities operating without meaningful legal constraint. Simultaneously, rights to bodily autonomy face legal rollback, and weapons capable of mass casualties are more accessible than ever. Real freedom requires confronting all of this directly.

What It Demands

  • Comprehensive federal privacy law — your data is yours, not a commodity
  • Algorithmic transparency and accountability for automated decision-making
  • Explicit protection of bodily autonomy as a constitutional right
  • Meaningful gun reform: universal background checks, red flag laws, restrictions on weapons designed for mass casualties
  • AI regulation with democratic oversight — not self-regulation by the companies building it

What It Rejects

  • Surveillance capitalism as a legitimate business model
  • Government use of commercially available data to surveil citizens without warrant
  • A healthcare system that makes reproductive decisions for patients
  • Unlimited access to weapons of war by private individuals
  • AI systems deployed at scale without accountability for their effects
Rights & Civil Liberties Gun Policy Technology & AI
Foundation V

Freedom to Thrive

The Value: Healthcare, a living wage, clean air and water, a livable planet — these are not luxuries. They are the material conditions without which every other freedom becomes theoretical. You cannot exercise any right while you are drowning.

Why It Matters: Roosevelt said it in 1944: necessitous men are not free men. The platform must have a material floor — a level of economic and physical security below which no one is allowed to fall. The alternative is a freedom that only the already-wealthy can use.

What It Demands

  • Universal healthcare access — no person bankrupted for getting sick
  • A living wage indexed to productivity and cost of living, actually enforced
  • Wealth taxation that prevents compounding fortunes from becoming permanent aristocracy
  • Climate policy that treats the environment as infrastructure, not an optional concern
  • A food system that produces food that nourishes people and supports the farmers who grow it

What It Rejects

  • Medical bankruptcy as an acceptable outcome in a wealthy nation
  • Wages that have not kept pace with worker productivity for fifty years
  • A tax code where the wealthiest pay lower effective rates than the working class
  • Environmental deregulation in service of short-term corporate profit
  • An agricultural system controlled by four companies that produces food optimized for shelf life rather than nutrition
Healthcare Taxation & Wealth Environment & Agriculture
