# Real Freedom — Policy Pillars

The following policy domains are built on this foundation:

## Rights and Civil Liberties
Makes rights explicit, modern, durable, and resistant to political rollback. Covers bodily autonomy, privacy, equal protection, freedom of conscience, digital rights, economic rights, and the structural protections that prevent rights from being eroded through judicial interpretation, legislative attack, or bureaucratic neglect. This pillar is shared with the Equal Justice foundation; its placement here reflects the liberty and autonomy dimensions of rights protection.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/rights_and_civil_liberties/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/rights_and_civil_liberties/policy.md)

## Gun Policy
Clarifies government authority to regulate firearms and armed groups while protecting lawful individual rights. Covers background checks, weapons-of-war prohibitions, private militia regulation, red flag laws, safety and training requirements, and the democratic accountability of armed organizations. The right to own a firearm does not require that every weapon, in every hand, without any safety condition, be beyond regulation.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/gun_policy/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/gun_policy/policy.md)

## Consumer Rights
Protects individuals from deceptive, predatory, and monopolistic practices in the marketplace. Covers right-to-repair, mandatory arbitration bans, data exploitation protections, product safety, and the principle that contractual power imbalances between corporations and individuals require affirmative legal correction. Real freedom requires the ability to own, repair, and use what you buy without corporate gatekeeping.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/consumer_rights/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/consumer_rights/policy.md)

## Technology and AI
Establishes governance frameworks for artificial intelligence, data systems, algorithmic accountability, and digital infrastructure. Covers AI transparency and safety, surveillance regulation, data privacy rights, platform accountability, and the protection of human rights and democratic values in the digital sphere. Technology that is ungoverned does not remain neutral — it concentrates power in whoever controls it.
