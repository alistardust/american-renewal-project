# Freedom to Thrive — Policy Pillars

The following policy domains are built on this foundation:

## Healthcare
Defines a comprehensive healthcare system that treats care as a guaranteed right, not a privilege. Covers universal access, single-payer transition, patient protections, drug pricing reform, mental health coverage, AI in healthcare, insurance accountability, and the structural and operational standards required to ensure that no person is denied medically necessary care because of cost, geography, or insurance status.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/healthcare/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/healthcare/policy.md)

## Taxation and Wealth
Establishes a genuinely progressive tax system designed to prevent extreme wealth concentration, support broad economic opportunity, and ensure that the gains of productivity are distributed across society rather than extracted upward. Covers progressive income and wealth taxation, automation and technology taxes, inheritance rules, corporate tax accountability, and the structural relationship between economic concentration and democratic equality.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/taxation_and_wealth/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/taxation_and_wealth/policy.md)

## Environment and Agriculture
Establishes comprehensive environmental protection, pollution control, biodiversity preservation, and sustainable agriculture frameworks. Covers climate policy, corporate environmental accountability, land and water protection, regenerative agriculture, and the principle that no person or corporation has the right to degrade the shared environmental commons for private profit. A livable environment is not a policy preference — it is the physical precondition for everything else.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/environment_and_agriculture/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/environment_and_agriculture/policy.md)
