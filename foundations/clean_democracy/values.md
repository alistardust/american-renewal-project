# Clean Democracy

## The Value

Government must answer to the people — not to corporations, not to wealthy donors, not to hidden networks of private power. Democracy is only real when votes count more than money, when elections reflect the will of citizens rather than the preferences of donors, and when public officials work for the public rather than for whoever funded their campaigns.

## Why It Matters

The system was not broken by any single decision. It was eroded over decades: by dark money that flows without disclosure, by lobbying that converts wealth into policy, by platform monopolies that control what people see and hear, by gerrymandering that lets politicians choose their voters instead of the other way around. The result is a democracy that is formally intact but functionally captured — where the machinery of elections exists but the outcomes are shaped before the voting begins.

When government answers to money rather than people, the corruption is not always visible. It does not always look like a bribe. More often it looks like access: a donor who gets the meeting, a lobbyist who gets the call returned, a corporation that gets the favorable rule, an industry that gets the regulator who used to work for them. The exchange is rarely explicit. But the pattern is consistent, and its effect is the same: public institutions serve private interests while appearing to serve everyone.

This matters because democracy is the only system under which people without money or institutional power can still have a voice. When that system is corrupted by money, the people who lose are those who cannot buy access. The wealthy can survive a corrupt government; they can often benefit from it. Working people cannot. Clean democracy is not a luxury reform — it is a prerequisite for everything else on this list.

## What It Demands

- Elections must be accessible, secure, and free from partisan manipulation — including through redistricting, voter suppression, or administrative obstruction.
- Dark money — political spending that hides its source — must be eliminated or rigorously disclosed.
- Lobbying must be transparent, and the revolving door between industry and government must be closed.
- Corporations and concentrated private wealth must not be permitted to dominate political campaigns or policy-making.
- Market monopolies, especially in media and information, must be broken up where they threaten democratic self-governance.
- The press must be free, and information infrastructure must be protected from coordinated manipulation.
- Anti-corruption enforcement must be independent, funded, and real — not a speed bump that sophisticated actors route around.
- Every eligible citizen must have an equal and accessible vote that counts equally in representation.

## What It Rejects

- Hidden political spending that conceals who is funding what and why.
- Partisan control over the administration of elections.
- The revolving door between regulatory agencies and the industries they oversee.
- Platform monopolies that function as the gatekeepers of public discourse while remaining unaccountable.
- Any system in which wealth translates directly into political access and policy outcomes.
