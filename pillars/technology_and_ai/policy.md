# Technology, AI, and Digital Rights — Policy Rules

## Scope Code: TEC

---

## AGE — Age Verification Systems

This family addresses age verification requirements for online content and services. The rules balance child safety concerns with privacy rights, prohibiting identity-based verification systems that function as mass surveillance infrastructure while allowing privacy-preserving alternatives.

### TEC-AGE-001 — Prohibit mandatory identity-based age verification
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Prohibit mandatory identity-based age verification systems that require government ID biometric data or persistent tracking to access lawful content

### TEC-AGE-002 — No centralized databases
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Age verification systems must not create centralized databases of identity or browsing activity

### TEC-AGE-003 — Allow privacy-preserving alternatives
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Allow age assurance mechanisms that preserve privacy such as local device verification zero-knowledge proofs or non-identifying credential systems

### TEC-AGE-004 — Data minimization and deletion
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Any permitted age verification system must minimize data collection and prohibit retention beyond immediate verification

### TEC-AGE-005 — Narrow scope requirements
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Age verification requirements must be narrowly scoped to specific high-risk content categories and may not be expanded to general internet access

### TEC-AGE-006 — Ban surveillance proxy use
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Governments and private entities may not use age verification systems as a proxy for surveillance censorship or behavior tracking

## AI — Artificial Intelligence Governance

This family establishes core governance principles for AI systems. High-risk AI affecting rights, liberty, healthcare, education, employment, or housing requires heightened oversight, transparency, human accountability, and testing for bias and safety.

### TEC-AI-001 — High-risk AI requires review
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** High-risk AI systems must be subject to heightened governance review before deployment

### TEC-AI-002 — No automation without accountability
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems that affect rights liberty healthcare education employment benefits policing or housing must not operate without meaningful human accountability

### TEC-AI-003 — Rights to notice and review
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** No fully automated decisions in critical domains without a right to notice explanation appeal and human review

### TEC-AI-004 — Transparency and auditability
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in public-sector decision-making must be documented transparent and independently auditable

### TEC-AI-005 — Liability for harms
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Operators and deployers of AI systems must retain legal liability for harms caused by those systems

### TEC-AI-006 — Testing for bias and safety
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must be tested for bias safety reliability and misuse risks before and after deployment

### TEC-AI-007 — Documentation disclosure
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Covered AI systems must maintain model cards documentation or equivalent public-facing disclosures appropriate to risk level

### TEC-AI-008 — No secret law
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Secret law or undisclosed AI decision systems must not be used to determine rights obligations or access to public services

## ALG — Algorithmic Accountability

This family governs algorithmic ranking, filtering, and recommendation systems. Users must be informed when algorithms influence decisions, have rights to contest harms, access non-algorithmic alternatives, and be protected from manipulative engagement optimization.

### TEC-ALG-001 — Disclosure of algorithmic influence
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** People must be told when AI or algorithmic systems materially influence ranking eligibility triage moderation or access decisions

### TEC-ALG-002 — Right to contest decisions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** People must have the right to contest materially harmful algorithmic decisions

### TEC-ALG-003 — Non-algorithmic alternatives
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Users must have access to non-algorithmic or less personalized alternatives where practical

### TEC-ALG-004 — Ban manipulative optimization
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban manipulative algorithmic optimization that targets psychological vulnerabilities for profit or political influence

### TEC-ALG-005 — Disclose ranking objectives
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Platforms must disclose core ranking and recommendation objectives in understandable terms

### TEC-ALG-006 — Protected researcher access
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Independent researchers must have protected access to platform data needed to study algorithmic harms subject to privacy safeguards

### TEC-AUD-001 — Independent auditing requirement

### TEC-AUD-002 — Meaningful redress for harms

### TEC-AUD-003 — Forensic logging
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Organizations deploying high-risk AI must keep logs sufficient for forensic review investigation and appeals

### TEC-AUD-004 — Public AI registry

### TEC-AUD-005 — Oversight of classified systems
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Classified or sensitive AI systems must still be subject to independent review under secure oversight procedures

## AUT — Automation and Autonomous Systems

This family restricts high-risk autonomous systems in contexts where failure could cause loss of liberty, bodily harm, or deprivation of rights. Human override authority and review are required.

### TEC-AUT-001 — Strict safeguards for high-risk systems
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** High-risk autonomous systems must not be deployed where failure could cause loss of liberty bodily harm or deprivation of rights without strict safeguards

### TEC-AUT-002 — No replacement of accountable humans
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in healthcare education benefits housing or employment decisions must not replace accountable human decision-makers

### TEC-AUT-003 — Prohibit automated denial systems
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Automated denial systems for benefits care housing or legal status must be prohibited unless human review and appeal rights are guaranteed before harm occurs

### TEC-AUT-004 — Human override authority
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government agencies must maintain human override authority and documented escalation procedures for automated systems

## BIO — Biometric Systems

This family restricts biometric surveillance including facial recognition, gait tracking, and real-time crowd monitoring. Mass biometric surveillance in public spaces is banned; where permitted, strict necessity, proportionality, and deletion rules apply.

### TEC-BIO-001 — Ban mass facial recognition
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban mass facial recognition in public spaces for general law-enforcement or administrative use

### TEC-BIO-002 — Ban real-time crowd tracking
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban real-time biometric tracking of crowds or demonstrations

### TEC-BIO-003 — No general identity infrastructure
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Biometric systems may not be used as general identity infrastructure for access to lawful content or public life

### TEC-BIO-004 — Strict safeguards where permitted
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Where biometrics are permitted they must require strict necessity proportionality auditability and deletion rules

### TEC-BIO-005 — Heightened data protection
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Biometric data must be treated as highly sensitive protected data with stronger consent and retention rules than ordinary personal data

## DAT — Data Rights and Surveillance Fusion

This family prohibits cross-agency fusion of surveillance datasets, creation of secret behavioral dossiers, and government use of commercially acquired bulk data.

### TEC-DAT-001 — Limit cross-agency surveillance fusion
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban or strictly limit cross-agency fusion of surveillance datasets unless specifically authorized by law subject to judicial oversight and publicly disclosed in general terms

### TEC-DAT-002 — Ban secret dossiers
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban creation of secret AI-generated behavioral dossiers on individuals absent individualized legal cause and judicial process

### TEC-DAT-003 — Ban government use of commercial data
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban use of commercially acquired bulk location biometric or behavioral data in AI surveillance systems

## EDU — Education Technology

This family governs AI in education. AI may assist but not replace human instruction, may not be the sole basis for grading, prohibits invasive surveillance and emotion inference, protects student data, and requires bias audits and transparency.

### TEC-EDU-001 — AI must enhance not replace learning
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems in education must enhance learning without replacing human instruction critical thinking or equitable access to education

### TEC-EDU-002 — No replacement of human educators
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not replace human educators in primary secondary or higher education contexts where direct instruction is required

### TEC-EDU-003 — Support not substitute
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may be used to support educators and students but may not substitute for qualified teaching or mentorship

### TEC-EDU-004 — No sole-basis grading
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be the sole basis for grading or evaluation in high-stakes academic decisions including advancement certification or disciplinary action

### TEC-EDU-005 — Right to human review
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Students have the right to human review of AI-influenced grading or evaluation decisions

### TEC-EDU-006 — Clear policies on AI use
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Educational institutions must establish clear policies on AI use that promote learning and prevent misuse without relying on unreliable detection systems

### TEC-EDU-007 — Evaluate for bias
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in education must be evaluated for bias and must not disadvantage students based on race gender disability socioeconomic status or language background

### TEC-EDU-008 — Accessibility and equity
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI tools used in education must be accessible to all students and may not create or reinforce educational inequality

### TEC-EDU-009 — Data minimization
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Student data collected by AI systems must be limited to what is necessary for educational purposes

### TEC-EDU-010 — No commercial use of student data
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Student data may not be sold shared or used for advertising profiling or unrelated commercial purposes

### TEC-EDU-011 — Enhanced protections for minors
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Enhanced privacy protections are required for minors using AI systems in educational settings

### TEC-EDU-012 — Ban invasive surveillance
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban use of AI systems for invasive surveillance of students including continuous monitoring behavioral tracking or biometric analysis without strict justification and safeguards

### TEC-EDU-013 — No emotion inference
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to infer student emotions attention or psychological state for evaluation or discipline

### TEC-EDU-014 — No behavioral scoring
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Educational institutions may not use AI systems to assign behavioral or compliance scores to students

### TEC-EDU-015 — Disclose AI-generated content
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI-generated educational content must be clearly identified and its limitations disclosed

### TEC-EDU-016 — No ideological manipulation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to impose ideological viewpoints or manipulate educational content without transparency and oversight

### TEC-EDU-017 — Support critical thinking
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Educational use of AI must support development of critical thinking rather than passive consumption of generated answers

### TEC-EDU-018 — Accessibility for disabilities
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI may be used to improve accessibility for students with disabilities including translation transcription and adaptive learning tools

### TEC-EDU-019 — Multilingual support
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems should support multilingual education and reduce language barriers without compromising accuracy or fairness

### TEC-EDU-020 — Disclosure requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Educational institutions must disclose use of AI systems in teaching evaluation and administration

### TEC-EDU-021 — Regular independent audits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in education must be subject to regular independent audits for bias accuracy and educational impact

### TEC-EDU-022 — No proprietary opacity
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Vendors providing AI systems to educational institutions may not use proprietary claims to avoid oversight or accountability

### TEC-EDU-023 — Demonstrate educational benefit
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must demonstrate educational benefit through evidence before widespread deployment in classrooms

### TEC-EDU-024 — Informed consent for testing
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Students may not be used as unwitting test subjects for AI systems without informed consent and appropriate safeguards

**Statement:** AI systems and infrastructure must not externalize environmental costs and must operate within sustainable limits for energy water materials and ecological impact

### TEC-ENV-002 — Carbon neutrality requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Large-scale AI systems and data centers must meet strict carbon neutrality or carbon-negative requirements over their full lifecycle

### TEC-ENV-003 — Energy disclosure
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Operators of large AI systems must publicly disclose energy usage sources emissions and efficiency metrics

### TEC-ENV-004 — Renewable energy supply
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** High-consumption AI infrastructure must supply or offset its energy usage through dedicated renewable or low-impact sources rather than relying on general grid extraction

### TEC-ENV-005 — Water usage disclosure
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI infrastructure operators must disclose water usage including cooling consumption and local environmental impact

### TEC-ENV-006 — Water resource protection
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must not disproportionately strain local water resources particularly in drought-prone or vulnerable regions

### TEC-ENV-007 — Materials sourcing standards
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Materials used in AI hardware including rare earth elements must be sourced under strict environmental and labor standards

### TEC-ENV-008 — Lifecycle responsibility
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI hardware producers must be responsible for full lifecycle impacts including manufacturing durability and end-of-life disposal

### TEC-ENV-009 — Durability and repairability
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI-related hardware must meet durability repairability and reuse standards to reduce electronic waste

### TEC-ENV-010 — Recycling programs
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Operators must implement responsible recycling and disposal programs for AI hardware and infrastructure components

### TEC-ENV-011 — Internalize environmental costs
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Companies developing or deploying AI systems must internalize environmental costs rather than shifting them to the public or ecosystems

### TEC-ENV-012 — Environmental impact assessments
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Large-scale AI deployments must undergo environmental impact assessments prior to construction or expansion

### TEC-ENV-013 — Support climate efforts
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI should be used to support climate modeling environmental monitoring and mitigation efforts under transparent and accountable frameworks

### TEC-ENV-014 — Resource optimization
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI may be used to optimize energy water and resource usage provided it does not create hidden environmental tradeoffs or inequitable impacts

### TEC-ENV-015 — Grid modernization support
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may support modernization of electrical grids microgrids and renewable energy systems while maintaining transparency and public accountability

### TEC-ENV-016 — No misrepresentation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI companies may not misrepresent environmental impact through selective reporting offsets or unverifiable sustainability claims

### TEC-ENV-017 — Standardized reporting
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Environmental reporting for AI systems must follow standardized verifiable metrics to prevent manipulation or selective disclosure

### TEC-ENV-018 — Environmental justice protection
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI infrastructure may not disproportionately locate environmental burdens in disadvantaged communities or regions

### TEC-ENV-019 — No global offloading
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Environmental costs of AI supply chains must not be offloaded to developing regions without equivalent protections and standards

### TEC-ENV-020 — Integration with national policy
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI infrastructure policy must integrate with national energy grid modernization renewable investment and public infrastructure strategies

### TEC-ENV-021 — Noise pollution standards
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Establish standards for noise pollution from AI data centers and other large data centers including monitoring mitigation and local community protections

## FIN — Financial Technology and AI

This family governs AI in finance, credit, and insurance. AI may not independently deny essential financial services; human review, transparency, fairness, and anti-discrimination protections are required.

### TEC-FIN-001 — Fairness and transparency in finance
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems in finance credit and insurance must not undermine fairness transparency equal access or protection from discrimination in essential economic systems

### TEC-FIN-002 — No automated denial of credit
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not independently deny credit loans mortgages refinancing or other essential financial services without direct human review and accountability

### TEC-FIN-003 — Human review before denial
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Any decision that would deny restrict or materially worsen access to credit or lending must be made directly and independently by a qualified human reviewer before harm occurs

### TEC-FIN-004 — No opaque criteria
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in underwriting or creditworthiness assessments may not rely on opaque or unexplainable criteria that materially affect outcomes

### TEC-FIN-005 — Anti-discrimination requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems in finance and insurance must not discriminate based on protected characteristics or proxies for those characteristics including zip code language behavior or network data

### TEC-FIN-006 — Regular audits for bias
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in lending underwriting pricing or claims decisions must be regularly audited for disparate impact and discriminatory outcomes

### TEC-FIN-007 — No automated insurance denial
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not independently deny restrict reduce or terminate insurance coverage or claims without direct and independent human review

### TEC-FIN-008 — Independent human judgment required
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Human reviewers in insurance decisions may not rely solely on AI-generated recommendations and must exercise independent judgment

### TEC-FIN-009 — AI for approval not denial
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may be used to assist or expedite approval of insurance claims or coverage but may not be used as the primary basis for denial restriction or reduction

### TEC-FIN-010 — No negative inference from AI
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Absence of AI approval or recommendation may not be used as evidence or implicit justification for denial restriction or delay of insurance claims or coverage

### TEC-FIN-011 — No behavioral surveillance scoring
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not use generalized behavioral surveillance or social scoring to determine eligibility pricing or access in finance credit or insurance

### TEC-FIN-012 — Transparent risk scoring
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Risk scoring in finance and insurance must be based on transparent lawful and relevant factors and may not rely on opaque behavioral inference

### TEC-FIN-013 — No vulnerability profiling
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not use individualized vulnerability profiling to impose exploitative pricing rates fees or terms

### TEC-FIN-014 — Fair housing access
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in mortgage housing finance or rental screening must not undermine fair access to housing or reproduce historical discrimination

### TEC-FIN-015 — Tenant screening limits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Tenant screening and housing-related AI systems may not rely on opaque scoring or unverifiable data that materially affect housing access

### TEC-FIN-016 — Right to explanation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** People have the right to a meaningful explanation of AI-influenced financial credit or insurance decisions affecting them

### TEC-FIN-017 — Right to appeal
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** People must have access to a timely human appeal process for materially adverse AI-influenced financial credit or insurance decisions

### TEC-FIN-018 — Disclosure requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Entities using AI in consequential financial or insurance decisions must disclose when such systems materially influence outcomes

### TEC-FIN-019 — Data minimization
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems in finance and insurance may collect only data that is strictly necessary for lawful and relevant decision-making

### TEC-FIN-020 — No data sales or repurposing
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Financial and insurance data used by AI systems may not be sold shared or repurposed for unrelated commercial targeting or behavioral profiling

### TEC-FIN-021 — No secret profile enrichment
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Entities may not secretly enrich financial or insurance profiles with third-party consumer data social graph data or surveillance-derived data without explicit lawful authority and disclosure

### TEC-FIN-022 — No cross-system denial loops
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to create cross-system denial loops where credit insurance housing or public-benefit decisions reinforce each other without independent review and due process

### TEC-FIN-023 — Independent audits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used in finance credit and insurance must be subject to regular independent audits for bias fairness legality and consumer harm

### TEC-FIN-024 — No proprietary opacity
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Trade secrecy or proprietary claims may not be used to prevent meaningful oversight explanation or appeal in consequential financial or insurance AI systems

### TEC-FIN-025 — Documentation requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Entities deploying consequential AI systems in finance and insurance must maintain documentation sufficient for review audit and accountability

### TEC-FIN-026 — No exploitation of vulnerability
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to identify and exploit financially vulnerable individuals through targeted loans fees insurance terms or sales practices

### TEC-FIN-027 — No impersonation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems in finance and insurance may not impersonate human advisors or misrepresent their authority capabilities or obligations to consumers

### TEC-FIN-028 — Public-interest standards for essential systems
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Where finance credit or insurance systems function as essential gateways to housing healthcare transportation or economic participation they must be governed by heightened public-interest standards

### TEC-FIN-029 — No automatic exclusion from essential systems
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must not be allowed to automatically exclude people from essential economic systems needed for basic social participation

## GOV — Government Use of AI

This family establishes comprehensive restrictions on government use of AI. AI may not independently deny, reduce, or terminate benefits or services; human review is required before harm; rights to explanation and appeal are guaranteed; behavioral scoring and forced AI-only channels are prohibited.

### TEC-GOV-001 — Preserve due process and rights
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used by government or public-service entities must not undermine due process equal protection transparency accountability or access to rights and services

### TEC-GOV-002 — No automated denial of benefits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not independently deny terminate reduce or suspend access to public benefits services or legal status determinations

### TEC-GOV-003 — Human decision-maker before harm
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Any decision that would deny reduce terminate or delay benefits services or rights must be made directly and independently by a qualified human decision-maker before harm occurs

### TEC-GOV-004 — Independent human judgment required
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Human reviewers may not rely solely on AI-generated recommendations when making public-service or benefits decisions and must exercise independent judgment

### TEC-GOV-005 — AI for approval not denial
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may be used to help identify likely eligibility or expedite approvals but may not be used as the primary basis for denial reduction or termination of benefits or services

### TEC-GOV-006 — Right to explanation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Individuals have the right to a meaningful explanation of any AI-influenced government decision affecting benefits services legal status or access to rights

### TEC-GOV-007 — Right to appeal
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Individuals must have a timely accessible appeal process before a human decision-maker for any materially adverse AI-influenced government decision

### TEC-GOV-008 — Disclosure requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government agencies must clearly disclose when AI systems materially influence decisions affecting the public

### TEC-GOV-009 — No behavioral scoring
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government may not use AI systems to assign generalized risk trustworthiness fraud or compliance scores to individuals for access to public services or rights

### TEC-GOV-010 — No discriminatory profiling
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government AI systems may not use protected characteristics or their proxies to infer eligibility suspicion dangerousness or worthiness

### TEC-GOV-011 — No social conformity scoring
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government may not use AI systems to monitor or score behavioral conformity ideological alignment or social desirability as a condition of access to services or benefits

### TEC-GOV-012 — Access to human representatives
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** People must retain access to human government representatives for matters involving benefits legal status healthcare housing education or essential public services

### TEC-GOV-013 — No forced AI-only channels
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government may not force individuals into AI-only service channels where lack of human access would impair fairness comprehension or ability to contest decisions

### TEC-GOV-014 — Accessibility for vulnerable populations
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI-enabled public services must be accessible to disabled low-income elderly and limited-English-proficiency users and may not create new access barriers

### TEC-GOV-015 — No automated disability denials
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to deny disability benefits or override licensed medical evidence without transparent human review and due process

### TEC-GOV-016 — No mass fraud sweeps
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government may not use AI systems to conduct mass fraud sweeps or suspicion-based benefits enforcement without individualized legal standards and human review

### TEC-GOV-017 — No automated termination of essential benefits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not automatically terminate housing food healthcare disability or income-support benefits based on scoring anomalies or probabilistic inference

### TEC-GOV-018 — No automated immigration decisions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not independently determine immigration status detention deportation asylum credibility or family reunification outcomes

### TEC-GOV-019 — No credibility inference in immigration
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government may not use AI systems to infer truthfulness credibility intent or dangerousness in immigration or asylum contexts

### TEC-GOV-020 — Human review for immigration decisions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Any AI-influenced immigration or detention decision must be reviewed and decided by a human official subject to due process and judicial oversight

### TEC-GOV-021 — Vendor accountability
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Private vendors and contractors supplying AI systems to government are subject to the same legal constitutional and ethical constraints as the government entities using them

### TEC-GOV-022 — No proprietary opacity
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Trade secrecy or proprietary claims may not be used to prevent meaningful oversight explanation or appeal for AI systems used in public decision-making

### TEC-GOV-023 — Procurement disclosure
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government procurement of AI systems must include public disclosure of purpose capabilities limitations oversight structure and vendor conflicts of interest

### TEC-GOV-024 — Regular independent audits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government AI systems must undergo regular independent audits for legality accuracy bias rights impacts and administrative fairness

### TEC-GOV-025 — Public AI registry
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** All materially consequential government AI systems must be listed in a public registry with clear descriptions of use authority and oversight

### TEC-GOV-026 — Sunset and reauthorization
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government AI systems affecting rights benefits or legal status must include sunset dates and periodic reauthorization requirements

### TEC-GOV-027 — Explicit legal authority required
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government agencies may not deploy consequential AI systems without explicit legal authority and defined limits on scope and use

### TEC-GOV-028 — No unwitting test subjects
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Government may not use the public as unwitting test subjects for consequential AI systems without lawful authority transparency and ethical safeguards

### TEC-GOV-029 — Right to challenge system legality
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Individuals must be able to challenge not only a government AI decision but also the legality and validity of the system used to make it

### TEC-IMM-002 — Ban risk scoring for detention
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-INT-004 — Common carrier treatment
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Internet service providers and core digital network infrastructure must be treated as common carriers or equivalent neutral service providers

### TEC-INT-005 — Non-discrimination requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Common carrier obligations must prohibit discrimination based on content source destination or political or competitive considerations

### TEC-INT-006 — No blocking or throttling
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Network operators may not prioritize degrade or block traffic for competitive advantage political influence or economic coercion

### TEC-INT-007 — Narrow technical exceptions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Net neutrality frameworks must allow narrowly scoped exceptions for technical optimization security reliability and research and development

### TEC-INT-008 — Transparent exceptions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** All exceptions to neutrality must be transparent auditable and prohibited from conferring unfair market or political advantage

### TEC-INT-009 — Protection from rollback
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Core neutrality and access protections must be insulated from administrative rollback through regulatory or executive action alone

## JUD — Judicial and Legal Systems

This family comprehensively restricts AI in courts and legal proceedings. AI may not determine sentencing, bail, punishment, or credibility; generative AI is banned in evidence and judicial reasoning; strict admissibility standards apply; extensive protections in family court, eviction, probation/parole, and administrative hearings.

### TEC-JUD-001 — No AI sentencing
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to determine sentencing bail or punishment in criminal justice proceedings

### TEC-JUD-002 — No risk scoring
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not assign risk scores for recidivism dangerousness or likelihood of compliance in judicial decision-making

### TEC-MIL-033 — Support international treaties

### TEC-MIL-034 — Treaty goal: ban autonomous weapons
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-MIL-035 — Treaty goal: ban targeting AI

### TEC-MIL-036 — Treaty goal: ban nuclear AI
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-MIL-037 — Treaty mechanisms
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-MIL-038 — Prevent AI arms races

### TEC-MIL-041 — Sanctions for prohibited systems

### TEC-MIL-042 — Narrow reciprocal exceptions

### TEC-MIL-044 — Core prohibitions remain
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-MIL-047 — Violations of armed conflict principles

### TEC-MIL-048 — Precision guidance exception
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-MIL-049 — Commanders remain liable

### TEC-MIL-053 — Minimize civilian cyber impact

### TEC-MIL-054 — Cyber branch oversight
**Status:** MISSING

### TEC-MIL-055 — No domestic cyber surveillance

### TEC-MIL-056 — Coordination with civilian agencies

### TEC-MIL-057 — International cyber norms

## SYN — Synthetic Media and Deepfakes

This family bans deceptive use of synthetic media for fraud, impersonation, non-consensual sexual content, political manipulation, and false depiction causing harm. Disclosure and provenance markers are required; parody and consensual use are allowed.

### TEC-SYN-001 — Ban deceptive synthetic media
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Synthetic media must not be used to deceive the public about real people or events in ways that cause harm, manipulate democratic processes, or undermine individual rights

### TEC-SYN-002 — Ban synthetic impersonation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban the use of AI-generated media to impersonate a real person for fraud, financial gain, or unauthorized access

### TEC-SYN-003 — Ban identity verification bypass
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban use of synthetic media to bypass identity verification systems

### TEC-SYN-004 — Ban non-consensual sexual content
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban creation and distribution of non-consensual synthetic sexual content depicting real individuals

### TEC-SYN-005 — Ban false harmful depiction
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban synthetic media that falsely depicts a real person saying or doing something in a way that would reasonably be understood as fact and would harm their reputation or safety

### TEC-SYN-006 — Ban political manipulation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban use of synthetic media in political advertising or messaging that depicts real individuals engaging in fabricated actions or speech

### TEC-SYN-007 — Ban misleading about officials
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban synthetic media used to materially mislead the public about the health, actions, or status of public officials

### TEC-SYN-008 — Ban coordinated deception
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Ban coordinated use of synthetic media to mislead the public about real-world events, elections, or public safety

### TEC-SYN-009 — Disclosure requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Require clear disclosure when media is substantially AI-generated or altered to depict realistic human activity

### TEC-SYN-010 — Provenance markers required
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Require AI-generated video and audio to include persistent, tamper-resistant provenance markers

### TEC-SYN-011 — Open-source detection
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Provenance markers must be detectable using publicly available, open-source, and free tools

### TEC-SYN-012 — Safeguards against removal
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Developers of generative media systems must implement reasonable safeguards to prevent removal or circumvention of provenance markers

### TEC-SYN-013 — Allow parody and satire
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Allow synthetic media for parody, satire, or artistic expression where it is clearly labeled or would not reasonably be interpreted as factual

### TEC-SYN-014 — Allow disclosed journalism use
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Allow use of synthetic media in journalism or documentary contexts when clearly disclosed and not used to mislead about real events

### TEC-SYN-015 — Allow consensual use
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Allow use of synthetic media depicting real individuals with explicit informed consent
