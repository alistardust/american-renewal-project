# Checks and Balances — Policy Rules

## Scope Codes: SYS (Systemic Structure), OVR (Oversight Mechanisms)

---

## OVR-BRN — Branch Oversight and Ethics

### OVR-BRN-001 — Independent Oversight Boards for Each Branch
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Each branch of government and major constitutional body must establish an independent criminal and ethics oversight board.

**Notes:** Creates structural requirement for independent oversight of each branch. Boards must have genuine independence—separate funding, appointment process that prevents capture, investigative authority, subpoena power, and public reporting requirements. Covers executive branch (including White House, Cabinet departments, agencies), legislative branch (both chambers of Congress), and judicial branch (federal judiciary including Supreme Court). Also applies to major constitutional bodies (such as Federal Reserve, independent agencies, commissions).

**Oversight authority includes:** Criminal conduct investigation, ethics violations, abuse of office, conflicts of interest, misuse of authority, corruption. Boards can refer matters for prosecution, issue public reports, recommend sanctions, and trigger mandatory review processes.

---

### OVR-BRN-002 — Oversight Board Independence
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Oversight boards must be independent from the bodies they oversee.

**Notes:** Structural independence requirements include: funding separate from overseen body (see OVR-FND rules), appointment process involving multiple authorities (not solely controlled by overseen body), protection from removal except for cause with independent review, prohibition on members serving in overseen body or having financial interests in it, subpoena and investigative authority that cannot be blocked by overseen body, public reporting requirements that cannot be suppressed or edited by overseen body.

**Failure modes prevented:** Oversight boards that are captured by the institutions they oversee, funding cuts to retaliate for aggressive oversight, removal of members who pursue politically sensitive investigations, blocking of subpoenas or access to information, suppression of reports that embarrass officials, appointment of loyalists who will not exercise oversight authority.

---

## OVR-FED — Federal Oversight Authority

### OVR-FED-001 — Federal Independent Oversight Body
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Establish a federal independent oversight and investigations body.

**Notes:** Creates federal-level oversight entity with broad authority to investigate any federally elected official and jurisdiction over high-level federal officials and certain state officials (see OVR-JUR rules). This is in addition to branch-specific oversight boards—it provides cross-branch capacity and ensures no official is beyond accountability. Model could be based on inspector general concept but with stronger independence, broader jurisdiction, and constitutional rather than statutory authority.

**Composition must ensure independence:** Members appointed through process involving multiple branches/authorities, elected component (see OVR-FED-003), terms that do not align with presidential or congressional terms, protection from removal except for cause.

---

### OVR-FED-002 — Authority Over Federally Elected Officials
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Federal oversight body has authority to investigate any federally elected official.

**Notes:** Jurisdiction includes President, Vice President, Cabinet members, Senators, Representatives, and federal judges. No official is exempt from investigation. This addresses the accountability gap where officials resist oversight by claiming political targeting or separation of powers. The oversight body is independent and empowered to investigate regardless of party, position, or political pressure.

**Investigative authority includes:** Conduct that may constitute crimes, ethics violations, abuse of office, conflicts of interest, misuse of public resources, corruption, obstruction of oversight. Authority includes subpoena power, access to records and communications (with appropriate safeguards for legitimate executive privilege and classified information), depositions, and witness interviews.

---

### OVR-FED-003 — Oversight Body Composition and Elected Component
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Federal oversight body includes one elected member and members appointed by each branch of government.

**Notes:** Composition designed to prevent capture by any single branch or party. Includes: one member directly elected (national election or election by state legislatures), members appointed by executive branch, members appointed by legislative branch (House and Senate may have separate appointees or joint process), member(s) nominated by judicial branch or appointed through judicial process. Terms should be staggered and longer than typical political cycles to insulate from immediate political pressure.

**Elected component rationale:** Provides democratic accountability and public voice in oversight. Elected member represents public interest in accountability and cannot be removed by officials being overseen. Creates direct connection between citizenry and oversight function.

---

### OVR-FED-004 — Subpoena and Deposition Powers
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Federal oversight body has subpoena and deposition powers.

**Notes:** Authority to compel testimony, documents, records, and other evidence. Subpoenas enforceable through federal courts. Failure to comply constitutes contempt with both civil and criminal penalties. Executive privilege and other claims can be litigated but do not automatically block oversight—burden is on officials to justify non-compliance in court.

**Scope includes:** Financial records, communications, internal documents, testimony from officials and staff, access to facilities and systems, expert analysis of records and evidence. Oversight body can refer matters to prosecutors but also issues public reports and recommendations.

## OVR-FND — Oversight Funding and Resources

### OVR-FND-001 — Guaranteed Adequate Funding
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Funding for oversight and investigation bodies must be guaranteed and adequate.

**Notes:** Oversight bodies require sufficient resources to perform their functions effectively. Funding must be adequate for: staff salaries and benefits, investigative resources (forensic accountants, legal experts, technical specialists), facilities and technology, legal costs (litigation support when subpoenas are challenged), public reporting and communication. Adequacy determined by function and workload, not by political preference of appropriators.

**Prevents weaponization through budget:** Oversight bodies that lack resources cannot conduct meaningful investigations. The guaranteed funding mechanism prevents the "starve oversight" strategy where uncomfortable investigations are blocked by cutting budgets.

---

### OVR-FND-002 — Automatic Extension if Funding Not Passed
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** If funding is not passed oversight bodies automatically receive a 6-month extension.

**Notes:** If Congress fails to pass funding for oversight bodies by the required deadline, funding automatically extends for six months at the prior year's level plus 10% increase (see OVR-FND-003). This creates incentive structure: pass adequate funding or face automatic funding at higher level. Prevents shutdown of oversight as budget weapon. Extension can repeat if funding continues to be blocked—oversight bodies remain funded regardless of appropriations gridlock.

**Mechanism design:** Automatic extension must be self-executing and not dependent on any official's action. Courts can enforce if officials attempt to block automatic funding. Creates proper incentive alignment: cooperate in funding process or accept automatic increase.

---

### OVR-FND-003 — Automatic Extension Includes 10% Increase
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Automatic funding extension includes a 10 percent increase.

**Notes:** The 10% increase serves multiple purposes: accounts for inflation and increased workload, creates stronger incentive to pass funding through normal process (accepting automatic funding means accepting increase), prevents strategy of deliberately blocking funding to freeze oversight at inadequate levels. Increase is cumulative if multiple extensions occur—repeatedly blocking funding results in progressively higher automatic funding.

**Prevents gaming:** Without the increase, officials could simply never pass oversight funding and accept perpetual extensions at flat level. The increase makes that strategy costly and creates pressure to engage in appropriations process.

---

### OVR-FND-004 — Funding Separate from Overseen Departments
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Oversight funding must be separate from the departments they oversee.

**Notes:** Oversight bodies cannot be funded through appropriations for the departments they oversee. Creates independent line items in budget process. Prevents departments from retaliating through budget process and prevents conflicts of interest where oversight might be softened to protect departmental funding.

**Applies to all oversight:** Branch-specific oversight boards funded separately from their branches, federal oversight body funded independently, ethics bodies funded separately from legislative or executive appropriations that could create dependencies.

---

### OVR-FND-005 — Protection from Political and External Influence
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Oversight funding must be protected from political and external influence.

**Notes:** Funding process and levels should be insulated from political retaliation for investigations. Specific protections include: multi-year appropriations or mandatory spending status (rather than annual discretionary appropriations), formula-based funding tied to objective metrics (workload, jurisdiction size, complexity), protection from line-item cuts targeting specific investigations or reports, transparency requirements for any proposed funding reductions (must justify and document reasoning publicly).

**External influence prevention:** Oversight bodies cannot accept private funding, donations, or grants that could create conflicts of interest. All funding through public appropriations with transparency.

---

## OVR-JUR — Oversight Jurisdiction

### OVR-JUR-001 — Jurisdiction Over State Officials
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Federal oversight body may investigate governors and high-level state officials.

**Notes:** Extends federal oversight jurisdiction to state governors, lieutenant governors, attorneys general, and other high-level state officials when conduct relates to federal matters, constitutional violations, or systematic failures of state oversight. This is not general federal supervision of states—it is targeted authority to investigate when state-level accountability mechanisms fail or when conduct affects federal interests.

**Triggers for federal jurisdiction:** Federal program administration (Medicaid, education funding, disaster response), constitutional rights violations, election integrity matters where state officials interfere with federal elections, failure of state oversight boards to perform duties, systematic corruption affecting federal programs or interstate commerce.

---

### OVR-JUR-002 — Jurisdiction Over Failed State Oversight
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Federal oversight body may investigate state oversight boards if they fail to perform duties.

**Notes:** When state oversight boards fail to investigate misconduct, become captured by officials they oversee, or are blocked from functioning by state-level political interference, federal oversight can step in. This is backstop authority—federal oversight does not replace state oversight but ensures that accountability exists even when state mechanisms are compromised.

**Standards for intervention:** Pattern of non-investigation despite credible allegations, evidence of political interference or capture, systematic underfunding or resource denial, blocking of access to evidence or witnesses, suppression of reports or findings.

---

## OVR-STA — State Oversight Requirements

### OVR-STA-001 — State Oversight Boards Required
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Each state must establish its own independent oversight board.

**Notes:** Parallel to federal requirement for branch oversight (OVR-BRN-001), each state must establish independent oversight board with jurisdiction over state elected officials and high-level appointees. Board must have independence, investigative authority, subpoena power, and public reporting capacity. States have flexibility in exact structure but must meet minimum standards for independence and authority.

**Jurisdiction includes:** Governor, lieutenant governor, state legislators, state supreme court justices, attorneys general, other constitutional officers, high-level agency officials. Authority includes criminal investigation referrals, ethics enforcement, abuse of office investigations, corruption cases.

---

### OVR-STA-002 — Directly Elected Members
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** State oversight boards include directly elected members.

**Notes:** Ensures democratic accountability and public voice in state oversight. At least some members must be directly elected by state voters. Elected members cannot be removed by state officials being overseen. Provides independence from appointment process that could be controlled by those subject to oversight.

**Rationale:** Appointed-only boards can be captured through appointment process. Elected component creates direct accountability to public rather than to officials. In states with strong one-party control, elected oversight members may be only genuinely independent check on abuse.

---

### OVR-STA-003 — Appointed Members Balance
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** State oversight boards include members appointed by legislatures and governors.

**Notes:** In addition to elected members (OVR-STA-002), boards include appointed members to ensure expertise and representation of different branches. Appointment process should involve: governor appointees, legislative appointees (potentially separate House and Senate appointments in bicameral states), potentially judicial appointments or appointments by independent commissions. Process prevents any single official or party from controlling appointments.

**Balance goal:** Combination of elected and appointed members creates board that is both publicly accountable and representative of multiple governmental interests. No single faction can dominate.

## SYS-AI — AI and Algorithmic Systems in Government

### SYS-AI-001 — Prohibition on Asymmetrical AI Processes
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** AI-assisted decision systems must not create asymmetrical processes that accelerate approvals while increasing delay scrutiny or denial likelihood for unapproved cases.

**Notes:** Addresses the two-tier problem where AI creates fast track for "normal" cases and slow track with heightened scrutiny for "abnormal" cases. If AI is used to speed approvals, it cannot simultaneously be used to flag cases for denial without explicitly requiring that equal efficiency be applied to all cases. Everyone receives equal processing speed and scrutiny level regardless of AI scoring.

**Failure mode prevented:** Benefits system that auto-approves cases the algorithm likes but sends "suspicious" cases (often correlated with race, disability, poverty markers) to intensive review that results in high denial rates. Criminal justice system that fast-tracks plea deals the algorithm recommends but flags other cases for enhanced prosecution. Immigration system that quickly processes some applications but delays others flagged by algorithm.

**Enforcement:** Auditing of processing times, approval/denial rates, and scrutiny levels across AI score categories. Disparate outcomes trigger investigation and corrective action.

---

### SYS-AI-002 — Independent Human Judgment Required for Material Harm
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Any decision that materially harms restricts or denies rights opportunities or access must be made through independent human judgment and may not rely on absence of AI support.

**Notes:** Consequential decisions require genuine human review. "Independent judgment" means reviewer examines evidence, applies legal and policy standards, documents reasoning, and makes decision based on merits—not based on AI recommendation or absence thereof. Reviewer must have authority to override AI and cannot be penalized for doing so.

**Material harm includes:** Denial of benefits, loss of rights, restriction of access, adverse determination in licensing or permitting, criminal charging decisions, immigration detention, child welfare interventions, termination of services.

**Key principle:** Absence of AI support is not evidence of problem or justification for denial. If algorithm does not flag case for approval, that tells you nothing about whether approval is warranted—it only tells you the algorithm didn't process it in the automated approval pathway. Human reviewer must make fresh determination based on actual case facts and applicable standards.

---

### SYS-AI-003 — Absence of AI Recommendation Not Evidence of Denial
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Absence of AI recommendation or prioritization may not be used as evidence or implicit justification for denial restriction or adverse outcomes.

**Notes:** Reinforces SYS-AI-002 by specifically prohibiting use of "no AI approval" as grounds for denial. If system does not auto-approve, human reviewer cannot use that fact to justify denial. The reviewer must evaluate case on merits. Lack of algorithmic approval is procedural artifact, not substantive evidence.

**Practical effect:** Prevents automation bias where humans defer to algorithm. Prevents creation of presumption that cases flagged by AI must be denied. Requires decision-makers to articulate reasoning independent of algorithmic process.

**Enforcement:** Denial decisions must document substantive reasoning. Pattern of denials correlated with AI flagging without documented independent reasoning triggers investigation. Training must emphasize that AI scores are processing tools, not merit assessments.

---

### SYS-AI-004 — Equal and Timely Consideration Regardless of AI Score
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** All individuals must receive equal and timely consideration in decision-making systems regardless of AI prioritization or scoring.

**Notes:** Directly addresses queue-jumping and delay as discrimination. If AI prioritizes some cases for fast processing, all cases must receive comparably fast processing—or AI prioritization must be eliminated. Cannot create situation where algorithm-favored cases get decisions in days while others wait months.

**Timely consideration requirement:** Processing timelines must be based on statutory or regulatory requirements, not AI scoring. If law requires decision within 30 days, AI cannot create 5-day processing for some and 90-day processing for others.

**Equal consideration requirement:** Level of scrutiny, evidence required, and decision standards must be uniform regardless of AI scores. Cannot require more documentation or apply stricter standards to cases the algorithm flags.

---

### SYS-AI-005 — AI Bias Detection and Mitigation
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** AI systems used in decision-making processes must include mechanisms to detect measure and mitigate bias and must be explicitly used to improve fairness where possible.

**Notes:** Requires affirmative use of AI for fairness, not just efficiency. Systems must be designed with bias detection: demographic parity analysis, disparate impact testing, examination of proxy variables, evaluation of training data representativeness. Mitigation strategies must be implemented when bias is detected: retraining models, adjusting thresholds, removing biased features, manual review of high-disparity outcomes.

**"Improve fairness where possible" requirement:** AI can be used to detect patterns of human bias and flag cases for review when decisions appear inconsistent or discriminatory. The goal is not to eliminate AI but to ensure it serves fairness and equality rather than encoding existing discrimination.

---

### SYS-AI-006 — Continuous Auditing for Bias and Disparate Impact
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Decision-making systems using AI must be continuously audited for bias disparate impact and unfair outcomes with corrective action required.

**Notes:** One-time fairness assessment is insufficient because systems drift, training data changes, and feedback loops develop. Continuous auditing means regular (at least annual, preferably more frequent) analysis of outcomes by protected characteristics. When disparate impact is detected, corrective action is mandatory—not optional or subject to cost-benefit balancing.

**Auditing requirements:** Outcome rates by race, gender, disability, age, geography, and other protected classes. Processing times and scrutiny levels. Approval/denial patterns. Appeal and override rates. Comparison to baselines and expected distributions.

**Corrective action:** System adjustment, threshold changes, model retraining, increased human review, policy clarification, training updates. If system cannot be made fair, it must be discontinued or replaced.

---

### SYS-AI-007 — Domain-Specific AI Rules
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Each policy domain or pillar must define its own AI system rules consistent with these overarching principles and adapted to domain-specific risks.

**Notes:** The SYS-AI rules establish general principles. Each domain (healthcare, criminal justice, benefits administration, immigration, education, employment, housing, etc.) must develop specific rules that implement these principles with attention to domain-specific harms and risks.

**Examples of domain-specific needs:**
- Healthcare: Rules for AI in diagnosis, treatment recommendations, coverage determinations, triage (covered in Pillar 9 and ECO-INS rules in Pillar 12)
- Criminal justice: Rules for risk assessment, bail decisions, sentencing recommendations, parole determinations
- Benefits: Rules for eligibility determination, fraud detection, case prioritization
- Immigration: Rules for visa decisions, asylum adjudication, deportation proceedings
- Education: Rules for admissions, student assessment, resource allocation
- Employment: Rules for hiring algorithms, performance evaluation, termination decisions

**Consistency requirement:** Domain rules must align with overarching principles and cannot create exceptions that violate the general requirements for fairness, independent judgment, and equality.

## SYS-FED — Federalism and Federal-State Balance

### SYS-FED-001 — High-Risk Systems Not Fully Centralized
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** High-risk systems must not be fully centralized.

**Notes:** For systems where failure or capture would be catastrophic (elections, critical infrastructure oversight, judicial independence, rights protection), full federal centralization creates single point of failure. If that central authority is captured or corrupted, entire system fails at once. Distributed authority with federal standards provides resilience.

**Examples:** Elections administered by states with federal standards and oversight. Courts with federal and state systems. Rights protections at both federal and state level. Law enforcement with local, state, and federal jurisdiction. This creates redundancy—if one level fails, others can provide accountability and protection.

---

### SYS-FED-002 — Elections Remain State/Local Administered
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Elections remain state/local administered.

**Notes:** Consistent with SYS-FED-001, elections continue to be run by state and local authorities rather than centralized federally. This prevents single point of failure and maintains multiple layers of accountability. However, federal standards apply (see SYS-FED-003) to prevent state-level suppression or manipulation.

**Rationale:** If federal government administered all elections, capture of federal election authority would allow nationwide manipulation. Distributed administration means would-be authoritarians must capture thousands of local authorities—far more difficult. Creates resilience through redundancy.

---

### SYS-FED-003 — Federal Standards for Elections
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Federal sets standards but not direct control of elections.

**Notes:** Complements SYS-FED-002 by establishing federal role: set minimum standards for ballot access, voter registration, vote counting, certification, transparency, and accessibility. Prevent state-level suppression through federal floor below which states cannot fall. But federal government does not directly run elections—states and localities implement federal standards.

**Balance:** Federal standards prevent race to bottom and ensure minimum fairness. State/local administration prevents centralized capture. Oversight from multiple levels (federal, state, local, civil society) creates accountability.

---

### SYS-FED-004 — Multiple Independent Oversight Layers Required
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Multiple independent oversight layers required.

**Notes:** No single oversight body should be sole check on power. Multiple independent oversight mechanisms—federal, state, judicial, legislative, independent commissions, civil society watchdogs—create redundant accountability. If one layer is captured or fails, others can detect and respond to abuse.

**Application:** Elections oversight includes federal DOJ, state election officials, local election boards, courts, civil rights organizations, international observers, media, academic researchers. No single entity can unilaterally declare election valid or invalid—multiple independent assessments required.

**Extends beyond elections:** Criminal justice, healthcare, financial regulation, environmental protection, labor enforcement—all benefit from multiple oversight layers with independent authority and resources.

---

### SYS-FED-005 — Safeguards Against Both State and Federal Abuse
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Safeguards against both state and federal abuse.

**Notes:** Federalism balance requires protection against abuse at both levels. States can suppress rights, discriminate, and manipulate processes—federal oversight and standards prevent this. Federal government can overreach, centralize inappropriately, or become captured—state autonomy, judicial review, and distributed administration prevent this.

**Design principle:** Create tension between levels that prevents either from dominating inappropriately. Federal authority checks state abuse. State autonomy checks federal overreach. Neither level can act unilaterally without accountability from the other.

## SYS-GEO — Geographic Equality

### SYS-GEO-001 — Geography Must Not Determine Rights Access
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Geography must not determine access to rights or care.

**Notes:** Constitutional rights do not vary by state. You do not have different rights in Texas vs California vs New York. States cannot make federally guaranteed rights inaccessible through indirect means (closing facilities, criminalizing travel, restricting providers, defunding services). If state makes right inaccessible locally, cross-state access and travel support must be guaranteed.

**Application:** Abortion access, gender-affirming care, reproductive healthcare, voting rights, free speech, due process, equal protection—these are not subject to state veto. States can choose not to provide services themselves but cannot prevent residents from accessing services elsewhere.

---

### SYS-GEO-002 — National Baseline Must Be Uniform
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** National baseline must be uniform.

**Notes:** Federal constitutional and statutory protections establish a floor that applies uniformly across all states. States cannot fall below this baseline. Creates national standard for rights, healthcare access, economic protections, environmental standards, labor rights, education quality, and other fundamental guarantees.

**States can exceed, not undercut:** States may provide additional protections, more generous benefits, stricter environmental standards, stronger labor protections—but cannot reduce below federal baseline. This is federalism that protects rather than undermines rights.

---

### SYS-GEO-003 — States May Not Degrade Federal Rights
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** States may not degrade federally guaranteed rights.

**Notes:** Reinforces SYS-GEO-002 by explicitly prohibiting state degradation of federal rights. If federal law guarantees a right, state cannot eliminate, reduce, or make inaccessible that right. States cannot use police power, health and safety justifications, or moral arguments to override federal rights.

**Enforcement:** Federal courts enforce when states violate federal rights. Federal legislation can establish private rights of action. DOJ can bring enforcement actions. Individuals can sue for violations.

---

### SYS-GEO-004 — Cross-State Access Guaranteed
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Cross-state access must be guaranteed.

**Notes:** If state makes federally guaranteed service unavailable, residents must be able to access service in other states without interference. States cannot criminalize travel for healthcare, prosecute residents for out-of-state care, impose civil liability on providers in other states, or use private enforcement schemes to circumvent this requirement.

**Specific protections:** Right to travel for healthcare. Right to receive care in states where it is legal. Prohibition on prosecuting individuals or providers for lawful out-of-state care. Prohibition on private enforcement schemes (like Texas SB8) that attempt end-runs around cross-state access protections.

---

### SYS-GEO-005 — Travel Support When Local Care Unavailable
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Travel support required when local care unavailable.

**Notes:** If federally guaranteed healthcare or service is not locally accessible, government must provide travel support to enable access elsewhere. This can be federal funding, state funding, insurance coverage, or dedicated travel assistance programs. Ensures that geographic barriers and economic barriers do not combine to make rights theoretical rather than real.

**Rationale:** Right to travel is insufficient if you cannot afford travel. Rights inaccessible due to poverty are not real rights. Travel support ensures that constitutional protections are not nullified by geographic and economic circumstances.

## SYS-FND — Foundational System Principles

**Note:** The SYS-FND rules capture the project's origin story, animating principles, and structural commitments. These are meta-rules that define the framework itself rather than specific policy implementations.

### SYS-FND-001 — Platform as Policy and Strategy
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Platform is both a policy framework and long-term strategy.

**Notes:** This is not merely a list of policy proposals. It is comprehensive framework with communication strategy, coalition-building approach, movement logic, and long-term implementation pathway. Combines policy substance with political strategy for achieving adoption.

---

### SYS-FND-002 — Restore Checks and Balances
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Restore checks and balances.

**Notes:** Core mission of the project. Constitutional system has degraded—executive overreach, judicial lifetime appointments without accountability, congressional abdication of oversight, lack of enforcement mechanisms. Project aims to restore structural balance through constitutional amendments, statutory reforms, and institutional redesign.

---

### SYS-FND-003 — Modernize the Constitution
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Modernize the Constitution.

**Notes:** Constitution written for 18th century cannot address 21st century challenges without modernization. Technology, corporate power, digital privacy, AI, climate change, economic concentration, information manipulation—these require constitutional-level frameworks that do not currently exist. Modernization means adaptation to current reality while preserving foundational principles.

---

### SYS-FND-004 — Guarantee Universal Equal Rights
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Guarantee universal equal rights.

**Notes:** Rights must be explicit, universal, and enforceable. No person should have different rights based on identity, geography, or wealth. Project includes comprehensive rights framework (Pillar 5) that makes explicit what current Constitution leaves implicit or fragmented.

---

### SYS-FND-005 — Address Economic Inequality Structurally
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Address economic inequality structurally.

**Notes:** Economic inequality is not merely policy preference question—it is structural threat to democracy. Project addresses through progressive taxation (Pillar 12), antitrust enforcement (Pillar 11), labor rights, economic security guarantees, and constitutional limits on wealth concentration. Treats extreme inequality as incompatible with democratic equality.

---

### SYS-FND-006 — Address Modern Systemic Issues
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Address modern systemic issues not covered by current law.

**Notes:** Current legal framework has gaps: AI and algorithmic decision-making, digital privacy and surveillance, platform power and information manipulation, climate change and intergenerational harm, automation and labor displacement, genetic privacy and biotechnology, mass data collection and analysis. Project creates frameworks for these modern challenges.

---

### SYS-FND-007 — Make Technology Work for People
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Make technology work for people.

**Notes:** Technology should serve human flourishing, not extraction and control. AI should improve fairness, not encode bias. Platforms should enable communication, not manipulate behavior. Automation should reduce toil, not eliminate livelihoods. Data collection should serve individuals, not surveillance. Project establishes frameworks to ensure technology serves democratic and humanistic values.

**Status:** INCLUDED

### SYS-FND-018 — Core Values: Truth, Equality, Freedom, Dignity
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** Core values include truth equality freedom dignity.

**Notes:** Foundational values that guide all policy development:
- **Truth:** Information systems must serve truth-seeking, not manipulation. Facts matter. Reality cannot be negotiated.
- **Equality:** All people have equal moral worth and equal rights. No person is inherently superior or inferior to another.
- **Freedom:** Individuals must have meaningful freedom—not just absence of government interference but positive capacity to make real choices.
- **Dignity:** Systems must respect human dignity. People are not commodities, data points, or resources to be extracted.

## SYS-RUL — System Rule Integrity

### SYS-RUL-001 — System Rules Ensure Completeness and Alignment
**Status:** INCLUDED

**Statement:** System rules ensure completeness and alignment.

**Notes:** Meta-structural requirement that system rules across all pillars remain consistent, complete, and aligned. Prevents contradictions between pillars. Ensures new rules do not conflict with existing framework. Requires periodic review to identify gaps, contradictions, or misalignments.

**Implementation:** Regular cross-pillar analysis, consistency checking, gap analysis, redundancy elimination where appropriate (or deliberate reinforcement where redundancy serves resilience). When rules conflict, higher-level principles (SYS-FND) provide resolution framework.
