# Immigration Reform — Policy Rules

## Scope Code: IMM

---

## ACC — Access to Services

### IMM-ACC-001 — Access to Services
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Immigration status may affect specific program eligibility but may not be used to deny emergency care basic education or other core rights-protective systems guaranteed under law

### IMM-ACC-002 — Access to Services
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Children within United States jurisdiction must have meaningful access to education and basic developmental supports regardless of immigration status

### IMM-ACC-003 — Access to Services
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Local access to healthcare schooling identification support and legal process should be designed so immigrants are not forced into invisibility or exploitation by bureaucratic exclusion

### IMM-ACC-004 — Access to Services
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Public-service systems should provide clear multilingual navigation so immigrants can understand rights obligations and available resources without needing insider access

### IMM-ACC-005 — Access to Services
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Basic access to reporting crime seeking emergency help attending school or seeking healthcare should not trigger retaliatory immigration enforcement except under narrowly defined serious circumstances

### IMM-ACC-006 — Access to Services
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** State and local institutions may not use ordinary public-service access points as covert immigration-enforcement traps

## CON — Contractor Accountability

**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Private contractors involved in immigration processing transportation surveillance case management or service delivery must be subject to full public-law accountability standards

### IMM-CON-002 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Immigration contractors may not claim secrecy proprietary privilege or privatized status to avoid oversight disclosure or judicial review

### IMM-CON-003 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Contractors performing immigration-related functions must be subject to independent audit public reporting and enforceable penalties for abuse or noncompliance

### IMM-CON-004 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** No contractor compensation structure may reward detention volume removals denial rates speed-over-fairness or other perverse enforcement incentives

### IMM-CON-005 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Immigration-service contracts must prioritize rights protection quality of care competence and accountability rather than lowest-cost extraction models

### IMM-CON-006 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Government may not fragment immigration functions across contractors in ways that obscure responsibility for rights violations neglect abuse or wrongful detention

### IMM-CON-007 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** People harmed by immigration contractors must have full access to remedies records complaint systems and legal claims against both the contractor and the government entity responsible

### IMM-CON-008 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Independent oversight bodies must have full access to contractor-run facilities records communications incident logs and complaint data

### IMM-CON-009 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Immigration enforcement detention adjudication and core administrative functions may not be delegated to private contractors and must be performed by publicly accountable government entities

### IMM-CON-010 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Core immigration functions include detention custody transportation enforcement decision-making adjudication case management and access to legal or custodial records and may not be outsourced to private actors

### IMM-CON-011 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Limited use of contractors may be permitted for non-core support services such as facility maintenance translation technical infrastructure or food services where those functions do not involve custody authority decision-making or rights-impacting control

### IMM-CON-012 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Contractors may not exercise authority over individuals including detention control transport authority enforcement discretion or access to sensitive legal decision-making processes

### IMM-CON-013 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** No compensation structure may reward detention volume enforcement activity removals denial rates or any metric tied to deprivation of liberty or rights

### IMM-CON-014 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Government entities may not indirectly outsource core immigration functions through layered subcontracting partnerships or inter-agency agreements designed to evade contractor restrictions

### IMM-CON-015 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** All permitted contractors must be subject to full public-law accountability including transparency requirements records access audit authority and legal liability equivalent to government actors

### IMM-CON-016 — Contractor Accountability
**Status:** MISSING
**Statement:** Individuals harmed by contractor actions must have full legal recourse against both the contractor and the responsible government entity without procedural barriers or liability shields
