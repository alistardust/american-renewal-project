# Freedom to Thrive — Policy Pillars

The following policy domains are built on this foundation:

## Healthcare
Defines a comprehensive healthcare system that treats care as a guaranteed right, not a privilege. Covers universal access, single-payer transition, patient protections, drug pricing reform, mental health coverage, AI in healthcare, insurance accountability, and the structural and operational standards required to ensure that no person is denied medically necessary care because of cost, geography, or insurance status.
→ [overview.md](./healthcare/overview.md) | [policy rules](./healthcare/policy.md)

## Taxation and Wealth
Establishes a genuinely progressive tax system designed to prevent extreme wealth concentration, support broad economic opportunity, and ensure that the gains of productivity are distributed across society rather than extracted upward. Covers progressive income and wealth taxation, automation and technology taxes, inheritance rules, corporate tax accountability, and the structural relationship between economic concentration and democratic equality.
→ [overview.md](./taxation_and_wealth/overview.md) | [policy rules](./taxation_and_wealth/policy.md)

## Education
Establishes universal access to high-quality education as a guaranteed right, ensuring every person—regardless of wealth, geography, race, or disability—can access the learning they need for economic mobility, civic participation, and full human development. Covers structural inequity in school funding, predatory student lending, secular public schooling, vocational pathways, and the full pipeline from early childhood through higher education.
→ [overview.md](./education/overview.md) | [policy rules](./education/policy.md)

## Labor and Workers' Rights
Establishes the rights of workers to organize, bargain collectively, and be protected from exploitation, misclassification, and unsafe conditions. Covers union rights, minimum wage floors, sectoral bargaining, automation displacement protections, gig worker misclassification, and the structural relationship between worker power and broadly shared economic prosperity.
→ [overview.md](./labor_and_workers_rights/overview.md) | [policy rules](./labor_and_workers_rights/policy.md)

## Housing
Establishes access to safe, stable, and affordable housing as a right rather than a commodity. Covers tenant protections, anti-speculation controls, social and public housing investment, community land trusts, anti-discrimination in lending and rental markets, and the principle that housing is a human necessity that market dynamics alone will not and cannot adequately provide.
→ [overview.md](./housing/overview.md) | [policy rules](./housing/policy.md)
Establishes comprehensive environmental protection, pollution control, biodiversity preservation, and sustainable agriculture frameworks. Covers climate policy, corporate environmental accountability, land and water protection, regenerative agriculture, and the principle that no person or corporation has the right to degrade the shared environmental commons for private profit. A livable environment is not a policy preference — it is the physical precondition for everything else.
→ [overview.md](./environment_and_agriculture/overview.md) | [policy rules](./environment_and_agriculture/policy.md)

## Infrastructure and Public Goods
Establishes public investment in shared infrastructure — the electrical grid, water systems, broadband internet, public transportation, and other foundational systems — as a non-negotiable obligation of government. Covers universal access standards, public ownership options, anti-monopoly rules for essential utilities, and the principle that the infrastructure of modern life cannot be left to extraction by private capital.
→ [overview.md](./infrastructure_and_public_goods/overview.md) | [policy rules](./infrastructure_and_public_goods/policy.md)

## Science, Technology, and Space
Establishes public investment in scientific research, technological infrastructure, and responsible space policy as national imperatives. Covers federal R&D funding floors, open-access research publishing, STEM education investment, the democratic accountability of emerging technologies, and the governance of commercial and government space activity.
