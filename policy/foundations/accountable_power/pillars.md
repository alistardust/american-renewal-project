# Accountable Power — Policy Pillars

The following policy domains are built on this foundation:

## Executive Power
Prevents the presidency from becoming a quasi-monarchical office. Covers limits on presidential authority, emergency powers, self-dealing, pardon abuse, and the structural requirement that no executive can place themselves above the law.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/executive_power/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/executive_power/policy.md)

## Checks and Balances
Restores and strengthens the separation of powers across all three branches of government. Covers congressional oversight authority, federalism, independent inspectors general, and protections against institutional capture or consolidation of power.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/checks_and_balances/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/checks_and_balances/policy.md)

## Term Limits and Fitness for Office
Ensures that public office is not held indefinitely or beyond reasonable capacity. Covers congressional term limits, cognitive and physical fitness standards, and removal mechanisms for officeholders who can no longer serve effectively.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/term_limits_and_fitness/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/term_limits_and_fitness/policy.md)

## Courts and Judicial System
Governs how courts operate, how judges are selected and held accountable, and how the judiciary is protected from politicization and capture. Covers Supreme Court reform, mandatory judicial ethics codes, and the structural independence of courts.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/courts_and_judicial_system/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/courts_and_judicial_system/policy.md)

## Legislative Reform
Restores Congress as a functional, representative lawmaking body capable of checking executive overreach and enacting coherent policy. Covers Senate structural reform, filibuster reform, House expansion, anti-gridlock procedural mechanisms, and the capacity of Congress to legislate effectively rather than delegating its authority to the executive branch or courts by default.
→ [overview.md](../../pillars/legislative_reform/overview.md) | [policy rules](../../pillars/legislative_reform/policy.md)
