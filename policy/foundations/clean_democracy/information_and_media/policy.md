# Information and Media — Policy Rules

## Scope Codes: MED (Media), INF (Infrastructure)

---

## MED-PRS — Press Freedom and Journalist Protection

### MED-PRS-001 — Protect Journalists
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Protect journalists engaged in lawful newsgathering and reporting.

**Notes:** Establishes constitutional-level protection for journalistic activity. Protection is activity-based (what you do) rather than identity-based (who you are), ensuring coverage of independent and digital journalists.

---

### MED-PRS-002 — Protect Sources
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Protect confidential sources.

**Notes:** Federal shield law protection. Journalists cannot be compelled to reveal sources except under narrowly defined, high-threshold judicial standards. Includes anonymous sources and whistleblowers who provide information to the press.

---

### MED-PRS-003 — Limit Compelled Disclosure
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Limit compelled disclosure.

**Notes:** Government may not compel disclosure of sources, unpublished materials, or journalistic communications except under strict judicial review with high evidentiary threshold. Requires showing that information is essential, unavailable through other means, and that disclosure serves a compelling government interest that outweighs press freedom.

---

### MED-PRS-004 — Ban Retaliation Against Journalists
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Ban retaliation against journalists.

**Notes:** Prohibits retaliation, prosecution, or adverse action against journalists for publishing information of public interest, except in clearly defined and constitutionally limited cases (e.g., direct incitement to imminent violence, genuine national security harm with judicial review). Prevents weaponization of legal system against the press.

---

### MED-PRS-005 — Prevent Misuse of Secrecy Laws
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Prevent misuse of secrecy laws.

**Notes:** Prohibits use of classification, national security designations, or secrecy systems to conceal wrongdoing, corruption, illegality, or abuse of power. Classification authority cannot be used to avoid accountability or prevent oversight. Requires that secrecy serve legitimate security purposes, not institutional self-protection.

---

### MED-PRS-006 — Judicial Review Before Disclosure
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Judicial review before disclosure.

**Notes:** Mandatory independent judicial review before any government action to compel disclosure of journalistic materials, communications, or sources. Review must be by a court independent of the investigating or prosecuting authority. Requires adversarial process with representation for press interests.

---

### MED-PRS-007 — Limit Surveillance of Journalists
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Limit surveillance of journalists.

**Notes:** Strict limits on use of surveillance tools, metadata collection, subpoenas, or warrants targeting journalists or their communications. Requires heightened judicial scrutiny, showing of compelling need, and exhaustion of alternative investigative methods. Prohibits backdoor access through third-party service providers without judicial oversight.

---

### MED-PRS-008 — Include Independent Journalists
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Include independent journalists.

**Notes:** Explicitly extends all press freedom protections to independent journalists, digital media creators, freelance reporters, and non-traditional news organizations. Closes the legacy-media loophole where protections apply only to established institutional press. Activity-based standard: if you engage in journalism, you receive journalism protections.

---

### MED-PRS-009 — Transparency on Targeting Journalists
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Transparency on targeting journalists.

**Notes:** Requires reporting and transparency when government actions target journalists, news organizations, or journalistic communications. Annual public reporting (with appropriate security redactions) on number and type of actions taken. Creates oversight mechanism and accountability for press-freedom impacts.

## INF-BLD — Infrastructure Construction Standards

### INF-BLD-002 — Carbon-Neutral Infrastructure
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Require infrastructure systems and buildouts to be carbon neutral or carbon negative.

**Notes:** All new infrastructure construction—transportation systems, buildings, grid expansions—must meet carbon-neutral or carbon-negative standards. Applies to federally funded projects and sets national standard for state and local infrastructure development. Recognizes that infrastructure built today determines emissions trajectory for decades.

---

## INF-ENR — Energy Policy and Transition

### INF-ENR-004 — End Fossil Fuel Subsidies
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** End oil and coal subsidies.

**Notes:** Phases out direct and indirect subsidies to oil and coal industries. Includes tax preferences, direct payments, below-market leases, and regulatory exemptions that artificially reduce fossil fuel costs. Redirects public investment toward renewable energy and energy transition support for affected workers and communities.

---

### INF-ENR-005 — Guarantee Fossil Fuel Phaseout
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Guarantee phaseout of oil and coal for energy production.

**Notes:** Establishes binding timeline for ending oil and coal use in energy production. Requires transition planning, worker retraining programs, and economic support for communities dependent on fossil fuel extraction. Phaseout schedule must align with climate science requirements and provide adequate time for just transition.

---

## INF-GRD — Power Grid Requirements

### INF-GRD-003 — Carbon-Neutral Power Grid
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Require a carbon-neutral or carbon-negative power grid.

**Notes:** National power grid must transition to carbon-neutral or carbon-negative operation. Requires investment in renewable energy generation, grid modernization, energy storage, and transmission infrastructure. Grid design must support distributed generation, resilience, and equitable access.

---

## INF-RAIL — Rail System Modernization

### INF-RAIL-001 — Modernize Rail System
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Modernize the U.S. rail system.

**Notes:** Investment in rail infrastructure to meet modern safety, speed, and reliability standards. Includes track upgrades, signaling systems, electrification, and station improvements. Addresses decades of deferred maintenance and underinvestment in passenger rail.

---

### INF-RAIL-002 — Expand High-Speed Rail
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Expand high-speed rail.

**Notes:** Development of high-speed rail corridors connecting major metropolitan areas. Provides competitive alternative to short-haul flights and car travel. Reduces transportation costs, carbon emissions, and travel times. Requires sustained federal investment and interstate coordination.

---

## INF-TRN — Transportation System Development

### INF-TRN-001 — Expand Public Transportation
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Expand public transportation systems.

**Notes:** Investment in public transit infrastructure—buses, light rail, subway systems, commuter rail. Prioritizes underserved communities and addresses transit deserts where car ownership is mandatory. Improves frequency, reliability, and coverage to make public transit a viable option for daily commuting and essential travel.

---

### INF-TRN-002 — Prioritize Accessible Public Transit
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Prioritize reliable affordable and accessible public transportation in infrastructure planning.

**Notes:** Infrastructure planning must center public transit access rather than treating it as secondary to car infrastructure. Considers accessibility for disabled users, affordability for low-income riders, reliability for work commutes, and geographic coverage. Shifts planning paradigm from car-first to transit-first design.

---

### INF-TRN-003 — Phase Out Gasoline-Only Vehicles
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Phase out gasoline-only and internal-combustion-only passenger vehicles.

**Notes:** Establishes timeline for ending sale of new gasoline-only and internal-combustion-only passenger vehicles. Requires transition to electric, plug-in hybrid, or other low-emission alternatives. Includes support for charging infrastructure, consumer incentives, and protections for used vehicle markets during transition.

---

### INF-TRN-004 — Require Hybrid Capability in Transition
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Require at minimum plug-in hybrid capability during transition periods where full electrification is not yet feasible.

**Notes:** During transition to full electrification, vehicles must at minimum offer plug-in hybrid capability. Recognizes that charging infrastructure, grid capacity, and battery technology may require phased transition. Plug-in hybrid requirement ensures emissions reduction while maintaining vehicle utility during infrastructure buildout.

---

### INF-TRN-005 — Strict Fuel Efficiency Standards
**Status:** MISSING

**Statement:** Establish extremely strict fuel-efficiency standards during transition away from internal combustion vehicles.

**Notes:** For vehicles remaining in production during transition, fuel efficiency standards must be substantially stricter than current requirements. Reduces emissions impact during transition period. Standards must be based on available technology and tightened progressively as transition advances. Closes loopholes that allow continued production of inefficient vehicles.
