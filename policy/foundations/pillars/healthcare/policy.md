# Healthcare System Reform — Policy Rules

## Scope Code: HLT

This document contains all 184 healthcare policy rules organized by family code. These rules establish comprehensive standards for universal healthcare coverage, AI governance in healthcare, mental health parity, insurance reform, drug pricing and access, emergency services, telehealth, research priorities, and transition-state protections.

## AI — AI in Healthcare

Comprehensive governance framework for artificial intelligence systems in healthcare settings, including restrictions on automated denials, safety standards, bias mitigation, patient rights, and data protections.

### HLT-AI-001 — High-risk AI classification
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems in healthcare must be treated as high-risk systems requiring strict standards for safety efficacy accountability and patient protection

### HLT-AI-002 — AI as support, not replacement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may support but not replace licensed medical professionals in diagnosis treatment decisions or patient care where such care is medically required

### HLT-AI-003 — Clinical decision support limits
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may be used for clinical decision support only where they assist rather than substitute for physician judgment

### HLT-AI-004 — Evidence standards before deployment
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must meet strong evidence standards for safety efficacy and bias before being used in clinical settings

### HLT-AI-005 — Continuous monitoring requirement
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Deployed healthcare AI systems must be continuously monitored for errors bias and adverse outcomes

### HLT-AI-006 — No independent diagnosis or prescription
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not independently diagnose or prescribe treatment without qualified human oversight and accountability

### HLT-AI-007 — AI may not deny care
**Status:** UPDATED  
**Statement:** AI systems may not be used to deny restrict or limit medically necessary care; any decision that would result in denial must be made directly and independently by a qualified human reviewer

### HLT-AI-007A — Independent clinical judgment required
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Human reviewers must exercise independent clinical judgment and may not rely solely on AI-generated recommendations when making decisions that affect access to care

### HLT-AI-007B — AI for approval only, not denial
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may be used to assist or expedite approval of care but may not be used as the primary basis for denial restriction or reduction of care

### HLT-AI-007C — Prior human review for denials
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Human review must occur prior to any denial decision and may not be deferred to post-decision appeal processes

### HLT-AI-007D — Absence of AI approval not grounds for denial
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Absence of AI approval or recommendation may not be used as evidence justification or implicit basis for denial restriction or limitation of care

### HLT-AI-007E — Independent evaluation required for denials
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** All denial decisions must be based on independent clinical evaluation and evidence and may not rely on absence of AI support or AI-generated prioritization

### HLT-AI-007F — No systemic bias from AI prioritization
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems used to assist approvals must not create systemic bias in review processes that increases likelihood of denial for cases not flagged or prioritized by AI

### HLT-AI-007G — Equal human consideration for non-AI-flagged cases
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Case review systems must ensure that requests not prioritized or flagged by AI receive equal and timely human consideration

### HLT-AI-008 — No opaque triage decisions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not make opaque triage or prioritization decisions that materially affect access to care without explainability and oversight

### HLT-AI-009 — AI may not prioritize cost over outcomes
**Status:** UPDATED  
**Statement:** Coverage decision-making entities must not use AI systems to prioritize cost reduction over patient health outcomes or to override licensed clinical judgment

### HLT-AI-010 — Access to explanations and appeals
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Patients and providers must have access to explanations and appeal processes for AI-influenced coverage decisions

### HLT-AI-011 — Right to human care
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Patients have the right to receive care from qualified human providers and may not be forced into AI-only care pathways

### HLT-AI-012 — Informed consent and opt-out
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Patients must be informed when AI systems are used in their care and must be able to opt out where feasible

### HLT-AI-013 — Strict data protections
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Healthcare data used by AI systems must be treated as highly sensitive with strict protections against misuse or unauthorized access

### HLT-AI-014 — No commercial data exploitation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Healthcare data may not be used for advertising profiling or unrelated commercial purposes

### HLT-AI-015 — Training data ethical standards
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Training data for healthcare AI must meet strict ethical legal and consent standards including safeguards against re-identification

### HLT-AI-016 — Fail-safe design
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Healthcare AI systems must fail safely and defer to human providers when uncertain or operating outside validated scope

### HLT-AI-017 — No unwarranted certainty
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems may not present outputs with unwarranted certainty in clinical contexts where uncertainty materially affects outcomes

### HLT-AI-018 — Bias evaluation and mitigation
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Healthcare AI systems must be evaluated and mitigated for bias across race gender socioeconomic status and other protected characteristics

### HLT-AI-019 — No disparity worsening
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI deployment must not worsen disparities in healthcare access or outcomes

### HLT-AI-020 — AI for research acceleration
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI may be used to accelerate medical research and drug development provided it meets safety transparency and ethical standards

### HLT-AI-021 — Research funding for neglected conditions
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Increase funding for research into under-studied conditions treatments and medications including diseases that are historically neglected or lack commercial incentives

### HLT-AI-022 — Priority for underfunded diseases
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Public funding priorities must include diseases and conditions that are prevalent but underfunded including sexually transmitted infections chronic conditions and non-commercial research areas

### HLT-AI-023 — Science-based AI policy
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Healthcare AI policy and approvals must be grounded in transparent evidence-based science and protected from political ideological or commercial distortion

### HLT-AI-024 — Safe AI integration
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must be integrated into healthcare systems in ways that preserve safety accountability and continuity of care

### HLT-AI-025 — Interoperability with privacy protections
**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** Healthcare AI systems should support interoperability and data portability without compromising privacy or security

**Status:** MISSING  
**Statement:** AI systems must not be designed or used to re-identify individuals from anonymized mental health data

## End of Healthcare Policy Rules

**Total Rules:** 184  
**Family Codes:** 20 (ACC, AI, APL, COV, CST, EMP, EMS, MHC, NET, OVR, PAU, PHR, REB, RSR, RX, STD, SUP, TEL, TRL, TRN)

These rules establish the comprehensive framework for healthcare system reform, addressing structural design, coverage mandates, AI governance, mental health parity, insurance reform, drug access, emergency services, telehealth, research priorities, and transition-state protections. Together they define a healthcare system that treats healthcare as a guaranteed right, protects patients from denial and delay, and builds toward universal single-payer coverage.
