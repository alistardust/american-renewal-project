Homepage Redesign — Three Approaches

Same goal: lead with hope, feel like a movement, enough substance to convince. Different ways to get there.

A

Reorder & Rewrite

Keep all existing sections. Move the emotional closing ("You can't be free if you're sick...") to the hero. Rewrite the hero mission text in movement voice. Reorder: Hope → Stakes → Where you fit → Framework as evidence → Tour.

Least disruptive. Fastest. Risk: still section-heavy and may still feel like a framework briefing.

B

Cut & Sharpen

Remove PolicyOS from the homepage entirely (it lives on its own page). Compress the five foundations into a single short statement. Expand the "Get Involved" section and move it above the fold. Flow: Hope → Movement identity (2–3 sentences) → Your place in it → What we're building (brief) → Go deeper.

Tighter and more movement-like. Risk: PolicyOS disappears from first impressions; the framework-curious visitor may feel the site lacks depth until they click through.

C

Restructure Around the Visitor

Reorganize the page around three questions a visitor asks: "Is this for me?" → "What are you actually doing?" → "What can I do?" Each question gets a section. The framework (PolicyOS, foundations) becomes the answer to question 2 — the proof that there's a real plan, not just complaints.

Recommended. Puts the visitor at the center. Every section earns its place by answering a real question. More work to write, but the structure is honest about what a homepage is for.
